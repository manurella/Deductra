# Deductra Code of Conduct

## Commitment

Deductra is committed to a professional, welcoming, and safe community. Participation is open to people who engage in good faith and treat others with dignity, regardless of identity, background, experience, ability, or viewpoint.

## Expected conduct

Participants are expected to:

- communicate with respect and professional care;
- discuss ideas, evidence, and code without attacking people;
- give and receive constructive feedback in good faith;
- acknowledge mistakes, correct harm, and credit the work of others;
- protect confidential, personal, and security-sensitive information;
- respect project scope, review decisions, and community boundaries.

## Unacceptable conduct

Harassment, discrimination, intimidation, threats, sexualized attention, deliberate disruption, impersonation, repeated unwanted contact, and disclosure of another person's private information are not acceptable. Abuse of project infrastructure or reporting channels is also prohibited.

## Scope

This policy applies in repository discussions, issues, pull requests, reviews, releases, and other spaces where someone is representing Deductra.

## Reporting and enforcement

Report possible violations privately to the [verified repository owner](https://github.com/manurella). If you do not already have a private channel, open an issue containing only a request to establish one; do not include incident details or personal information in that issue.

The repository owner will review reports as confidentially and impartially as practicable. Responses may include clarification, a warning, temporary participation limits, removal of content, or a permanent ban. The response will be proportionate to the behavior, its impact, and any repeated pattern.

Retaliation against a person who makes a good-faith report or assists an investigation is prohibited.
