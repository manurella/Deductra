# Project Risk Register

Last reviewed: 2026-07-16

Probability and impact use the values Low, Medium, and High. The repository owner is accountable for review until responsibility is delegated publicly.

| ID | Risk | Probability | Impact | Leading indicator | Mitigation | Contingency | Owner | Review point |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| R-001 | A bounded packet expands into unreviewed later-stage implementation. | Medium | High | Modules or behavior appear outside the active packet. | Keep changes bounded and review current-state scope in every pull request. | Revert or separate the out-of-scope change. | [@manurella](https://github.com/manurella) | Every packet |
| R-002 | Dependency or workflow supply-chain compromise. | Low | High | Unexpected lock changes, mutable action references, or audit findings. | Lock dependencies, pin actions to full SHAs, run dependency review and scheduled audits. | Block release, revoke credentials, restore trusted pins, and publish an advisory when required. | [@manurella](https://github.com/manurella) | Every dependency change |
| R-003 | Python 3.14 ecosystem compatibility regresses. | Medium | Medium | Compatibility CI or container builds fail on Python 3.14. | Test Python 3.13 and 3.14 and keep the runtime dependency set minimal. | Hold the affected update or document a time-bounded compatibility exception. | [@manurella](https://github.com/manurella) | Every dependency update |
| R-004 | Documentation and implementation drift apart. | Medium | Medium | Broken links, stale current state, or contracts changed without documentation. | Run documentation checks in local hooks and pull-request CI. | Correct the canonical document in the same change that resolves the drift. | [@manurella](https://github.com/manurella) | Every contract change |
| R-005 | Sensitive or temporary material enters public history. | Low | High | Repository-hygiene review or secret scanning finds an unexpected artifact. | Maintain generic ignore rules, local-only exclusions, and explicit contributor guidance. | Revoke exposed secrets immediately and follow an evidence-preserving history-remediation plan. | [@manurella](https://github.com/manurella) | Every pull request |
| R-006 | A release workflow publishes unintended or unverified artifacts. | Low | High | A tag does not match project version or is not reachable from `main`. | Restrict publication to version tags, verify ancestry and version, and attest artifacts. | Remove affected artifacts, revoke the release, correct the tag through documented recovery, and investigate. | [@manurella](https://github.com/manurella) | Every release |
| R-007 | Direct changes can reach `main`, and private vulnerability reports are unavailable. | Medium | High | An unreviewed push lands or a reporter avoids public disclosure without a private channel. | Use disciplined pull requests and the published security contact while repository controls are deferred. | Enable branch protection and private vulnerability reporting when the owner chooses to adopt the controls. | [@manurella](https://github.com/manurella) | Each milestone |
| R-008 | Persisted events or indexed stream metadata diverge or are altered. | Low | High | Chain verification, representation checks, or replay fail. | Store canonical envelopes with indexed identity and hashes; verify before reads and appends; keep the log append-only. | Quarantine the stream, preserve evidence, restore from a verified source, and investigate before projection or reporting. | [@manurella](https://github.com/manurella) | Every event or migration change |
| R-009 | A state projection, branch record, or snapshot diverges from canonical event history. | Low | High | Source hashes, replay equivalence, branch retention, or snapshot verification fail. | Require immutable hashed states, exact source-state continuity, complete replay tests, and non-authoritative snapshots. | Discard derived projections, quarantine inconsistent artifacts, and rebuild from a verified genesis event stream. | [@manurella](https://github.com/manurella) | Every reducer or snapshot change |
| R-010 | Solver defects, native dependency regressions, or backend disagreement authorize an invalid deduction. | Low | High | A compatibility smoke test fails, a backend returns invalid or unknown, or satisfiability results disagree. | Maintain independent encodings, exact source binding, locked dependencies, cross-version CI, and fail-closed coordinator outcomes. | Block state reduction and release, retain and quarantine certificates, then investigate both encodings and dependency versions. | [@manurella](https://github.com/manurella) | Every verification or dependency change |
| R-011 | A human rule emits an invalid deduction, nondeterministic ordering, or undisclosed search. | Medium | High | Repeated inputs produce different trace hashes, a proposal cites stale evidence, or a human-rule event originates from search. | Validate rule contracts, use total policy ordering, require verification authority, and make exhaustion explicit. | Reject the proposal without changing state, quarantine disagreement, and disable the offending rule version until corrected. | [@manurella](https://github.com/manurella) | Every rule or policy change |
| R-012 | A projected hypergraph becomes nondeterministic, loses evidence, or is treated as mutable authority. | Low | High | Repeated projection changes identifiers, incidence references are unresolved, or a consumer writes graph state back into canonical history. | Derive stable identifiers from immutable sources, enforce closure and graph hashes, and keep projection dependencies read-only. | Reject the projection, rebuild from verified sources, and correct or disable the offending consumer. | [@manurella](https://github.com/manurella) | Every graph-contract change |
| R-013 | A generated candidate is accepted from incomplete, conflicting, fabricated, or non-reproducible evidence. | Medium | High | A playable result lacks a unique proof, canonical trace, matching difficulty, fingerprints, novelty evidence, or valid lineage. | Keep evaluator implementations behind typed ports, validate the event chain and request identity, quarantine uncertainty, and allow puzzle payloads only on full acceptance. | Remove the candidate from play, preserve its lineage and evidence, quarantine the producing adapter, and independently repeat every hard gate. | [@manurella](https://github.com/manurella) | Every generation-contract or evaluator change |
| R-014 | A memory projection drifts from source events or is mistaken for canonical evidence. | Medium | High | Clean replay changes projection hashes, direct index writes appear, or an inference lacks exact supporting event references. | Use typed hash-chained source streams, pure deterministic reducers, full-replay equivalence checks, and evidence-only learning aggregates. | Delete and rebuild affected views, quarantine invalid source streams, and block consumers until hashes and event references agree. | [@manurella](https://github.com/manurella) | Every memory-projection change |
